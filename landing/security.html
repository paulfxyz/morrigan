Cryptographic Foundation

Built on provable
cryptographic primitives.

Every component of Morrigan's security model is based on peer-reviewed, publicly audited algorithms. No proprietary ciphers. No homegrown schemes. Just the gold standard.

Authenticated encryption
XChaCha20-Poly1305

Poly1305 AEAD authenticated encryption. Best-in-class stream cipher with 192-bit nonce — eliminates nonce reuse risk vs AES-GCM.

Key derivation
Argon2id

Memory-hard key derivation. Your passphrase → vault key. Resistant to GPU and ASIC attacks. The passphrase never crosses the network.

Key splitting
Shamir SSS

Information-theoretically secure secret sharing. Split your key into N shares. Require any M to reconstruct.

Encryption Model

How your vault is protected.

Four layers of cryptographic protection, each independently verifiable. No single point of failure.

🔐
Client-Side Encryption

libsodium.js runs entirely in your browser. Your vault key is derived from your passphrase using Argon2id — locally. The key never leaves your device. Only ciphertext is sent to our servers.

🔑
XChaCha20-Poly1305

A 256-bit authenticated symmetric cipher. Each item is encrypted with a unique random nonce (192-bit). The Poly1305 MAC ensures tamper detection — any modification to the ciphertext is detected and rejected.

⛓
Bitcoin Timestamping

Your vault hash is committed to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps. This creates a tamper-evident proof of existence that is verifiable by any Bitcoin node — entirely independent of Morrigan's servers.

🔢
Argon2id KDF

When you create your vault, your passphrase is stretched through Argon2id (memory-hard, time-hard). The resulting key never leaves your browser. If an attacker steals the database, they get only ciphertext — brute-forcing requires enormous compute per guess.

3-Channel Identity

One compromised channel
is never enough.

Morrigan provides three independent identity channels for every account. For any sensitive action, at least 2 of your 3 registered channels must confirm. This is 2-of-3 multi-factor authentication applied to every critical operation.

We cannot reset or recover your passphrase — by design. If you lose it, access to your vault is permanently lost. This is the unavoidable tradeoff of zero-knowledge encryption. We strongly recommend storing your passphrase using a password manager and distributing Shamir shares to trusted people.
