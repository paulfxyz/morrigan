Security

Morrigan is built on the premise that security is not a feature — it is the product. This page documents our threat model, our cryptographic choices, and how to report vulnerabilities.

Adversary Model

Threat model

Understanding what Morrigan protects against — and what it does not — is essential for using it responsibly.

What we protect against
  • Guards Mass surveillance and passive network monitoring — all data is encrypted before transmission
  • Guards Data breaches at the server level — we store only ciphertext we cannot decrypt
  • Guards Insider threats — zero-knowledge architecture means no employee can access your vault
  • Guards Government compulsion orders — we have nothing to hand over but encrypted blobs
What we do not protect against
  • Limit Physical coercion ("rubber hose" attacks) — we cannot protect you if someone forces you to reveal your key
  • Limit User-side malware — if your device is compromised before encryption, Morrigan cannot help
  • Limit Loss of key without backup — there is no recovery mechanism; this is by design

"If you lose your key, your data is irrecoverable. No reset. No backdoor. No exceptions. That's not a limitation — it's the guarantee."

Cryptographic architecture

Every cryptographic primitive is chosen for its security properties, auditability, and long-term viability. No proprietary algorithms. No custom implementations.

Randomness CSPRNG Web Crypto API OS-seeded crypto.getRandomValues() — never Math.random() None known for this config

All cryptographic operations run client-side. The server receives and stores only ciphertext. Algorithm choices are reviewed annually and may be updated as the landscape evolves. Changes are announced in the public changelog.

Vulnerability Reporting

Responsible disclosure

We follow coordinated disclosure. Researchers have a 90-day deadline before public disclosure. We will never make legal threats against good-faith security researchers.

1
Email our security team

Send details of the vulnerability to security@morrigan.life. Include: description, reproduction steps, potential impact, and your contact preferences.

2
We acknowledge within 48 hours

You will receive a confirmation that we have received and are reviewing your report. We will provide a tracking reference number.

3
Confirmation & fix within 14 days

We will confirm whether the report constitutes a valid vulnerability and provide a timeline for the fix. We may ask follow-up questions.

4
Researcher credit

We credit all researchers by name in the security advisory, unless they prefer to remain anonymous. We will ask your preference before publishing.

5
Public security advisory

After the fix is deployed, we publish a full security advisory including: vulnerability description, affected versions, CVSS score, timeline, and researcher credit.

Found a vulnerability?

If you believe you have discovered a security issue in Morrigan, we want to hear from you. We review all reports promptly and take every submission seriously.

Common Questions

Security FAQ

Honest answers to the questions we hear most often about Morrigan's security model.

Can Morrigan employees access my data?

No. Morrigan's zero-knowledge architecture means we store only ciphertext that we cannot decrypt. Your encryption key never leaves your device and is never transmitted to our servers. Even if a Morrigan employee wanted to read your vault, they would encounter only an opaque encrypted blob — mathematically indistinguishable from random data without your key.

What happens if Morrigan shuts down?

Your encrypted vault can be exported at any time from within the application. The vault format is fully documented and open — it is not proprietary. You can decrypt your vault offline using our open-source CLI tool, which will always be available on GitHub regardless of Morrigan's operational status. We deliberately designed for this scenario: you should never be dependent on our continued existence to access your own data.

Is the code audited?

Not yet formally. Morrigan is fully open source and the codebase is available for public review on GitHub. We are actively seeking funding for a formal third-party security audit and intend to conduct one before a public launch. We believe in transparency over false confidence: it is better to acknowledge the current state honestly than to imply assurances that have not yet been independently verified.

What if I lose my key?

Your data is irrecoverable. This is not a limitation — it is the core guarantee of the system. If Morrigan could recover your data, so could a court order, a data breach, or a rogue employee. The irreversibility is the security property. We strongly recommend printing your key and storing physical copies in at least two separate secure locations (e.g. a home safe and a trusted family member's). Shamir's Secret Sharing can distribute key shards to trusted people so that no single loss event is catastrophic.

Do you store IP addresses or metadata?

Minimal metadata is collected for operational purposes — specifically: request timestamps, vault size (to enforce storage limits), and authentication events. We never log plaintext content, encryption keys, or key shards. IP addresses are processed transiently by our infrastructure but are not stored in association with your account. Our full data retention policy is documented in the Privacy Policy.
