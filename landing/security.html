Architecture

How your vault is protected.

Three principles guide every architectural decision we make.

💻

Client-Side Only

Encryption and decryption occur entirely within your browser using the Web Cryptography API and libsodium.js. Your passphrase and plaintext never cross the network boundary.

🚫

Zero Server Knowledge

Our servers receive only ciphertext and metadata. We cannot read vault contents, cannot reset passphrases, and cannot assist with decryption — by design, not by policy.

🔍

Open for Audit

Every line of code is public on GitHub. Security researchers, cryptographers, and users can inspect, audit, and verify every claim we make about the system's behaviour.

Cryptography

The cryptographic stack, explained.

No marketing language. No hand-waving. Here is exactly what we use and why.

XChaCha20-Poly1305 is an authenticated encryption with associated data (AEAD) cipher. It combines the XChaCha20 stream cipher with the Poly1305 MAC. The extended nonce variant (192-bit) eliminates random nonce collision risk, making it safe to generate nonces randomly for any practical lifetime of the application. It is audited as part of libsodium, used by WireGuard, and is a TLS 1.3 cipher suite. Unlike AES-GCM, it does not degrade under hardware that lacks AES acceleration and has no known related-nonce attacks.

Argon2id won the Password Hashing Competition in 2015 and is the OWASP-recommended algorithm for password hashing and key derivation. Morrigan uses Argon2id with 64MB memory requirement and 3 iterations. The memory-hard property means that an attacker who wants to brute-force your passphrase must pay 64MB of RAM per guess — making GPU farms and ASICs economically impractical. The "id" variant combines data-dependent and data-independent memory access patterns, resisting both side-channel and time-memory trade-off attacks.

Shamir's Secret Sharing (SSS) is an information-theoretically secure scheme. Your vault key is split into N shares such that any M shares can reconstruct the secret, but M−1 shares reveal absolutely zero information. This is not just computationally secure — it is provably impossible to recover anything with fewer than M shares, regardless of compute power. Morrigan uses this to enable vault recovery without a centralised key escrow. Recommended: 2-of-3 or 3-of-5 configurations distributed to family members, a lawyer, and a trusted friend.

OpenTimestamps is an open-source protocol that creates Bitcoin-anchored timestamp proofs. Morrigan computes a SHA-256 hash of your encrypted vault, aggregates it into a Merkle tree with thousands of other hashes, and commits the Merkle root to a Bitcoin block. The resulting .ots proof file allows any observer to independently verify that your specific vault hash existed before a given Bitcoin block timestamp. The proof does not require Morrigan's existence to verify — only a Bitcoin node. This creates a legally meaningful, tamper-evident record entirely independent of our infrastructure.

bcrypt is limited to 72-byte inputs and has a static memory cost (4KB), making it vulnerable to modern GPU attacks. scrypt is more memory-hard but has poor parallelism resistance. Argon2id is specifically designed to defeat three classes of attacker simultaneously: GPU farms (memory-hard), ASICs (memory-hard + time-memory tradeoff resistance), and side-channel attacks (data-independent memory access). It was selected after an international competition with over 20 candidates. For key derivation — not just password storage — Argon2id is the only recommendation from OWASP, NIST SP 800-63B, and the Argon2 team.

Identity

Three channels. Two required.

Morrigan provides three independent identity channels — Email OTP, Phone SMS, and TOTP authenticator. At least two must confirm before any sensitive action proceeds. One compromised channel is never enough.

✉️

Channel 1 — Email OTP

A time-limited one-time code sent to your verified email address. Confirms you control the account's primary identity anchor.

📱

Channel 2 — Phone SMS

An OTP delivered via SMS to your registered phone number. Physically separate from your email — compromising email alone is not enough.

🔑

Channel 3 — TOTP 2FA

Time-based one-time passwords from any open-source authenticator app — Aegis, Raivo, Google Authenticator, Bitwarden, and others. Works offline. Most phishing-resistant of the three channels.

2-of-3 required confirmations

Any two of the three registered channels must confirm. You choose which two.

Blockchain

Bitcoin as your notary.

A permanently timestamped, tamper-evident record of your vault's existence — anchored in the most secure public ledger ever created.

1

Hash

A SHA-256 hash is computed from your encrypted vault ciphertext. The hash is a fingerprint — unique to your exact vault contents — without revealing anything about the underlying data.

2

Merkle Tree

Your hash is combined with thousands of other hashes into a Merkle tree. The tree root is a single value representing all hashes, allowing efficient proof of inclusion without storing all hashes on-chain.

3

Bitcoin Anchor

The Merkle root is committed to a Bitcoin block via an OP_RETURN transaction. Bitcoin's proof-of-work consensus means the anchor is immutable and timestamped by the global network.

4

Proof Receipt

You receive a .ots proof file. Anyone — with only a Bitcoin node — can verify that your vault hash existed before a given block time. The proof does not require Morrigan to exist.

Post-Quantum

Quantum-resistant by roadmap.

Quantum computers will render RSA and ECC obsolete within this decade. Morrigan is already building the migration path.

🔷
v0.5.0

Kyber-1024

NIST-selected KEM (CRYSTALS-Kyber). Provides 256-bit post-quantum security for key encapsulation. Replaces ECDH for key exchange with a hybrid classical+PQ scheme.

✍️
v0.5.0

Dilithium

NIST-selected digital signature scheme (CRYSTALS-Dilithium). Lattice-based. Replaces ECDSA for vault signing and integrity verification.

🌳
v0.6.0

SPHINCS+

Hash-based signature scheme with minimal security assumptions. Stateless and conservative — security relies only on hash function properties, not lattice hardness.

🔒
v0.6.0

HQC

Backup KEM based on error-correcting codes. Provides algorithm diversity — independent of lattice hardness assumptions, offering a second post-quantum path.

📋

A non-profit charter commitment

Morrigan is a non-profit foundation. Our charter includes an explicit obligation to upgrade to the strongest available encryption standards as the threat landscape evolves. This is not aspirational — it is a founding obligation. When quantum computers make today's algorithms obsolete, we are legally committed to migrating all vaults to post-quantum alternatives, at no cost to users.
