● Cryptographic Stack

Encryption you can audit.

Every algorithm is open-source, battle-tested, and replaceable. We follow NIST standards and will always upgrade.

XChaCha20-Poly1305

Primary symmetric encryption. 256-bit key, 192-bit nonce. AEAD construction. Constant-time implementation.

Argon2id

Key derivation from user passphrase. Memory-hard. Resistant to GPU and ASIC attacks. RFC 9106 compliant.

Shamir's Secret Sharing

Vault key splitting. M-of-N threshold scheme over a finite field. No partial information is revealed by fewer than M shares.

OpenTimestamps

Bitcoin-anchored proof of existence. SHA-256 hash aggregated into a Merkle tree, root submitted to Bitcoin. Default anchoring layer.

Kyber-1024

Post-quantum key encapsulation mechanism (KEM). NIST PQC standard. Module-LWE hardness assumption. Roadmap v0.5.0.

Dilithium

Post-quantum digital signatures. NIST PQC standard. Module-LWE + Module-SIS. Roadmap v0.5.0.

The Transformation

The Morrígan shifts form. She is never trapped in one state. So too must encryption evolve. As quantum computing threatens classical cryptography, Morrigan will migrate — Kyber for key encapsulation, Dilithium for signatures, SPHINCS+ for stateless hashing. We track the NIST PQC standards process and will always adopt the strongest available primitives. Our architecture is designed for cryptographic agility: swapping an algorithm is a configuration change, not a rewrite.

● How It Works

From account to immortality
in four steps.

01
Create Your Vault

Sign up with email + phone. Your identity is dual-anchored. A vault key is derived from your passphrase using Argon2id and never transmitted.

02
Add Your Secrets

Documents, messages, instructions, final wishes. Everything is encrypted instantly in your browser before being stored. We see only ciphertext.

03
Set Your Switch

Define check-in intervals and beneficiaries. Assign specific vault sections to specific people. You remain in full control for as long as you're alive.

04
Rest Easy

The chain runs silently. When you stop checking in, Morrigan waits the grace period, verifies, and delivers your legacy — exactly as you intended.

● Blockchain Proof

Every vault state is written in stone.

We hash your vault and anchor it to the Bitcoin blockchain via OpenTimestamps. Only a SHA-256 hash touches the chain — no personal data, ever.

STEP 01
🔢
Hash

Your vault content is SHA-256 hashed client-side. The hash is a fixed 32-byte fingerprint — mathematically irreversible.

STEP 02
🌲
Merkle Tree

Your hash is aggregated with others into a Merkle tree. This batches thousands of proofs into a single Bitcoin transaction.

STEP 03
⛓️
On-Chain

The Merkle root is embedded in a Bitcoin transaction via OP_RETURN. Immutable once confirmed. Auditable by anyone.

STEP 04
📄
Receipt

You receive a cryptographic proof file (.ots). Use it to verify your vault's existence at any point in time, independently of Morrigan.

● The Origin

Not a pitch deck. A question.

2019

My mentor in military intelligence was killed in Damascus. His knowledge, his counsel, his stories — gone without ceremony. There was no record. No letter. Nothing left behind intentionally.

2019, Christmas

My father died. This time closer to home, but the same silence. The things unsaid. The instructions never given. The love that lived only in memory — and no assurance it would survive mine.

2020

I started asking: what happens to the things we meant to say? What if there were a system — not a diary, not a will — but a cryptographically secure vault that could be trusted to deliver the right message to the right person at the right time?

2024

Morrigan was born. Named for the Celtic goddess of fate, death, and transformation — the one who sees what is coming and bears witness. She is not a product. She is an answer.

Paul Fleury
Founder · Morrigan Foundation
"Morrigan is not a product born from a pitch deck. It is an answer to a question I could not stop asking: what happens to the things we meant to say? To the final instructions, the confessions, the love letters, the explanations that grief prevents us from writing in time. I lost my mentor in Damascus. I lost my father at Christmas. I could not change those losses — but I could build something that ensures the words survive. That is what Morrigan is." — Paul Fleury, Founder
