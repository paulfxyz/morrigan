Why Morrigan

Death entered slowly, then all at once.

In the winter of 2019, my mentor was killed in Damascus. He was a man who had shaped how I saw the world — precise, humane, irreplaceable. ISIS took him without warning. That same Christmas, my father died.

Two losses in one year broke open something I'd carried without knowing: the comfortable illusion that there would be time. There wouldn't be time.

What I found myself asking wasn't philosophical. It was practical: what happens to the things we leave behind? My mentor left a lifetime of knowledge — inaccessible. My father left passwords on slips of paper nobody could find.

Morrigan is not a product born from a pitch deck. It is an answer to a question I could not stop asking: what happens to the things we meant to say?

— Paul Fleury, founder

Under the Hood

Cryptography worthy of what you're protecting.

Marketing words like "military-grade" are meaningless. Cryptographic choices are engineering decisions — they have consequences, tradeoffs, and histories. Morrigan doesn't hide behind abstractions. Here is exactly what we use, and why we chose it.

256-bit

XChaCha20-Poly1305

256-bit key, 192-bit nonce. Poly1305 MAC delivers authenticated encryption — integrity and confidentiality in one pass. Constant-time on every platform, no hardware dependency required.

libsodium crypto_secretstream
128-bit equiv.

Curve25519 / X25519

Elliptic-curve key exchange. 128-bit security at 3,000× the speed of RSA-2048. Used to encrypt key shards to each beneficiary's public key. The de facto modern standard.

libsodium crypto_box
128-bit equiv.

Ed25519

Deterministic digital signatures — no random nonce, no fault-attack surface. Used to sign vault manifests and cryptographically prove authorship. Immune to the ECDSA vulnerability class.

libsodium crypto_sign
64 MB memory

Argon2id

Winner of the Password Hashing Competition. Memory-hard against GPU and ASIC attacks. Parameters: time=3, memory=64MB, parallelism=4. Your passphrase is never stored — only its derived key.

argon2-browser PHC winner
256-bit secret

Shamir's Secret Sharing

A 256-bit secret split into N shares via Lagrange interpolation over GF(2⁸). Any K shares reconstruct it. K−1 shares reveal nothing. Independently audited implementation.

secrets.js-grempe GF(2⁸)
10 yrs audited

libsodium & TweetNaCl

The implementation layer. A decade of production hardening, used by Signal, WhatsApp, and Cloudflare. TweetNaCl.js for in-browser: minimal attack surface, no OpenSSL, no legacy ciphers.

Foundation Commitment

The stack transforms. Always.

Algorithms age. Paradigms shift. As a non-profit with no commercial pressure, Morrigan is constitutionally committed to one principle: we will always migrate to the strongest available cryptographic standards.

Not a roadmap item. A founding obligation.

NIST PQC Final
CRYSTALS-Kyber Key Encapsulation

Lattice-based KEM. Replaces X25519 for asymmetric key exchange. NIST FIPS 203 (ML-KEM). Secure against both classical and quantum adversaries.

NIST PQC Final
CRYSTALS-Dilithium Digital Signatures

Lattice-based signature scheme. Replaces Ed25519 for vault manifest signing. NIST FIPS 204 (ML-DSA). Compact signatures, fast verification.

NIST PQC Final
SPHINCS+ Hash-Based Signatures

Stateless hash-based fallback. No lattice assumptions — pure hash security. NIST FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA). The belt alongside the braces.

On watch
HQC & BIKE Code-Based Diversity

NIST Round 4 candidates. Code-based cryptography from a completely different mathematical family — redundancy against any single algorithm break.

XChaCha20-Poly1305 is symmetric and already quantum-resistant. Migration targets are asymmetric primitives only: X25519 → Kyber for key exchange, Ed25519 → Dilithium + SPHINCS+ for signatures.

Why not X?

Why not AES-GCM?

Requires hardware AES acceleration for constant-time operation. XChaCha20 is constant-time on every platform by design — no exceptions.

Why not RSA?

3072-bit keys for 128-bit security, significantly slower, no forward secrecy. X25519 matches the security level at a fraction of the cost.

Why not bcrypt or scrypt?

Argon2id dominates both. bcrypt maxes at 72-byte passwords. scrypt lacks side-channel resistance. Argon2id wins on both axes simultaneously.

All cryptographic code is open source and auditable.

Every line. No obfuscation. No proprietary black boxes. You can read, fork, and verify everything at github.com/paulfxyz/morrigan.

How It Works

Four steps to sovereign peace of mind

Morrigan makes cryptographic security human. The hard parts are handled automatically — you stay in control of the meaning.

01

Create your vault

Choose a passphrase. Morrigan derives an encryption key via Argon2id — memory-hard, GPU-resistant. Your key never leaves your device.

Argon2id · client-side only
02

Name your beneficiaries

Assign trusted people. Each gets one shard of your key. You set how many shards are needed to unlock — no single person can act alone.

Shamir's Secret Sharing · K-of-N threshold
03

Store what matters

Upload files, write messages, add passwords. Everything is encrypted before upload. Our servers see only ciphertext.

XChaCha20-Poly1305 · BYOS compatible
04

Set the switch

Configure a dead man's switch. Miss a check-in and Morrigan notifies your beneficiaries. They combine shards. Your legacy is delivered.
