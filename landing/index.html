Why Morrigan

Built for the question
nobody wants to ask.

What happens to everything you've built — your accounts, your words, your assets — if you're suddenly gone? Morrigan is the answer. Open-source, zero-knowledge, and designed so we can never read your data even if we wanted to.

Features

Everything your legacy needs.

From zero-knowledge encryption to blockchain-anchored timestamps — every feature in Morrigan is built around one principle: your data is yours, and only yours.

🔐
Zero-Knowledge Vault

All encryption runs in your browser via libsodium.js. Your passphrase and plaintext never cross the network. We cannot read your vault — by design.

⏱
Dead Man's Switch

Set check-in intervals from daily to monthly. Miss a check-in, a grace period begins. After it expires, your designated beneficiaries receive their items — automatically.

👥
Beneficiary System

Assign specific vault items to specific people. A letter for your partner, credentials for your executor, photos for your children. Granular control over who gets what.

🛡
3-Channel Identity

Email OTP, phone SMS, and TOTP 2FA. Any two of your three channels must confirm sensitive actions. One compromised channel is never enough for an attacker.

₿
Bitcoin Anchoring

Every vault gets a Bitcoin-anchored timestamp via OpenTimestamps. A tamper-evident proof that your vault existed at a specific date — independent of Morrigan's infrastructure.

🔑
Shamir's Secret Sharing

Split your vault key into N shares. Require any M to reconstruct. Distribute to trusted people across jurisdictions. Information-theoretically secure — not just computationally.

How it works

Set up in minutes.
Protected forever.

1
Create your vault

Sign up, choose your passphrase. Your vault key is derived client-side with Argon2id — we never see it.

2
Add your legacy

Documents, messages, credentials, wishes. Encrypt them. Assign items to specific beneficiaries.

3
Set your switch

Choose your check-in interval and grace period. Check in to keep the switch off. Stop checking in and it fires.

4
They receive it

Your beneficiaries are notified and guided through identity verification to receive their items.

No. Encryption and decryption happen entirely in your browser using libsodium.js. Your passphrase is never sent over the network, and we store only the encrypted ciphertext. We have no mechanism to decrypt your vault — by design, not by policy. Even a full server compromise would expose only ciphertext.

You set a check-in interval (daily, weekly, or monthly). As long as you check in, nothing happens. If you miss a check-in, a configurable grace period begins — time for illness, travel, or accidents. Only after the grace period expires does your vault delivery sequence begin, notifying beneficiaries through their verified identity channels.

Morrigan provides three independent identity channels: email OTP, phone SMS, and TOTP authenticator (Aegis, Raivo, Google Authenticator, etc.). For any sensitive action, at least 2 of your 3 channels must confirm. This means compromising one channel — a hacked email, a stolen phone — is never sufficient for an attacker. And losing one channel doesn't lock you out.

The code is open-source and can be self-hosted. Your Bitcoin timestamp proofs are verifiable with any Bitcoin node — they don't depend on Morrigan's infrastructure.
