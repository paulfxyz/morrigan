Why Morrigan

Death entered slowly,
then all at once.

In the winter of 2019, my mentor was killed in Damascus. ISIS took him without warning. That same Christmas, my father died.

Two losses in one year broke open something I had carried without knowing — the comfortable illusion that there would be time. There would not be time.

What I found myself asking was not philosophical. It was practical: what happens to the things we leave behind? My mentor left a lifetime of knowledge — inaccessible. My father left passwords on slips of paper nobody could find.

We prepare for physical death — wills, estates, notaries. We do almost nothing to prepare for our digital selves. That, I have come to believe, is a failure of love as much as logistics.

Morrigan is not a product born from a pitch deck. It is an answer to a question I could not stop asking.

— Paul Fleury, founder

Under the Hood

Cryptography worthy of what you're protecting.

Marketing words like "military-grade" are meaningless. Here is exactly what we use, and why.

256-bit

XChaCha20-Poly1305

Primary cipher. 192-bit nonce — safe for random generation with negligible collision probability. Authenticated encryption: integrity and confidentiality in one pass. Constant-time on all hardware.

libsodium crypto_secretstream
128-bit

Curve25519 / X25519

Elliptic-curve key exchange. 128-bit security at 3,000× the speed of RSA-2048. Used to encrypt key shards to each beneficiary's public key.

libsodium crypto_box
128-bit

Ed25519

Deterministic digital signatures. No random nonce — no fault-attack surface. Signs vault manifests to cryptographically prove authorship of your instructions.

libsodium crypto_sign

Winner of the Password Hashing Competition. Memory-hard against GPU and ASIC attacks. Your passphrase is never stored — only its derived key. Irrecoverable even if servers are seized.

Foundation Commitment

The stack transforms. Always.

Algorithms age. Paradigms shift. As a non-profit with no commercial pressure, Morrigan is constitutionally committed: we will always migrate to the strongest available cryptographic standards.

Not a roadmap item. A founding obligation.

NIST Final CRYSTALS-Kyber Key Encapsulation

Lattice-based KEM. Replaces X25519. NIST FIPS 203 (ML-KEM).

NIST Final CRYSTALS-Dilithium Digital Signatures

Replaces Ed25519 for vault signing. NIST FIPS 204 (ML-DSA).

NIST Final SPHINCS+ Hash-Based Signatures

Stateless fallback. No lattice assumptions. FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA).

On watch HQC & BIKE Code-Based Diversity

NIST Round 4. Different mathematical family — redundancy against any single algorithm break.

XChaCha20-Poly1305 is symmetric and already quantum-resistant at 256-bit. Migration targets are asymmetric primitives only: X25519 → Kyber, Ed25519 → Dilithium + SPHINCS+.

Why not X?

Why not AES-GCM?

Requires hardware AES acceleration for constant-time operation. XChaCha20 is constant-time on every platform by design.

Why not RSA?

3072-bit keys for 128-bit security, significantly slower, no forward secrecy. X25519 matches security at a fraction of the cost.

Why not bcrypt or scrypt?

Argon2id dominates both. bcrypt maxes at 72-byte passwords. scrypt lacks side-channel resistance. Argon2id wins on both axes.

All cryptographic code is open source and auditable.

Every line. No obfuscation. No proprietary black boxes. Read, fork, and verify at github.com/paulfxyz/morrigan.

How It Works

Four steps to sovereign peace of mind.

The hard cryptographic parts happen automatically. You stay in control of the meaning.

01

Create your vault

Choose a passphrase. Morrigan derives an encryption key via Argon2id — memory-hard, GPU-resistant. Your key never leaves your device.

Argon2id · client-side only
02

Name your beneficiaries

Assign trusted people. Each receives one shard of your key. You set how many shards are needed to unlock — no single person can act alone.

Shamir's Secret Sharing · K-of-N threshold
03

Store what matters

Upload files, write messages, add passwords. Everything is encrypted before upload. Our servers see only ciphertext.

XChaCha20-Poly1305 · BYOS compatible
04

Set the switch

Configure a dead man's switch. Miss a check-in and Morrigan notifies your beneficiaries. They combine shards. Your legacy is delivered.

Dead man's switch · Configurable grace period
